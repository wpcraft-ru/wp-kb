## What is WordPress Marketing Automation

Systematic use of software tools and plugins to automatically execute marketing tasks based on predefined triggers and user behaviors. Three key components:
- Trigger events (form submissions, page visits, purchase completions)
- Conditional logic (determines what happens next)
- Automated actions (sending emails, updating profiles, adding tags)
- Integration capabilities (connecting different tools and platforms)

## Email Marketing Automation

- **Welcome series**: New subscribers receive greeting + best content
- **Drip campaigns**: Targeted content based on interests/behaviors
- **Abandoned cart recovery**: Reminders + discounts for incomplete purchases

## WooCommerce Marketing Automation Strategies

- **Product recommendation engines**: Suggest related items based on purchase history/browsing
- **Customer segmentation**: Different workflows for first-time buyers, repeat customers, high-value clients
- **Purchase-based triggers**: Onboarding emails for new customers, early access for existing
- **Win-back campaigns**: Special offers, new product announcements to re-engage dormant customers

## Common Challenges

- **Technical integration issues**: Plugin conflicts, API limitations
- **Over-automation**: Risk of losing personal touch
- **Data management**: Keeping customer info synchronized
- **Performance concerns**: Multiple plugins slowing down website
- **Compliance**: Data protection, email marketing regulations

## Measurement

- Email metrics: open rates, click-through rates, conversion rates
- Website analytics: time on site, pages per session, bounce rates
- Revenue attribution: connecting automation to business results
- Customer lifetime value
