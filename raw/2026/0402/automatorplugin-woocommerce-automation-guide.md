## Core Concept

WooCommerce automation uses triggers and actions to handle repetitive store tasks. Structure: **Triggers → Actions → Outcomes**

### Front-End Automation (customer-facing)
- **Awareness**: Track campaigns, tag users by landing page, push to social channels
- **Consideration**: Follow up on product views, send "compare options" emails, route pre-sales questions to Slack
- **Conversion**: Multi-channel abandoned cart sequences, time-sensitive coupons, payment error notifications
- **Loyalty**: Post-purchase follow-ups, rewards programs, re-order reminders
- **Advocacy**: Review requests, referral programs, "share your photo" emails

### Back-End Automation (operational)
- **Sales reporting**: Auto-generate daily/weekly reports, log to Google Sheets, AI-powered insights
- **Inventory**: Low-stock alerts, supplier notifications, auto-hide out-of-stock products
- **Order processing**: Route to warehouse, internal notifications, tracking number updates
- **Customer support**: Auto-tag refund requests, assign to right person, surface order details
- **Accounting**: Auto-sync orders, refunds, taxes, invoices to accounting platform
- **Subscriptions**: Failed payment handling, retry schedules, team notifications
