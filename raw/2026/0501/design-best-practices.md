# Design Best Practices

This support guide will give you a few tips on creating a website that utilizes the best web design practices to realize your vision of creating a beautiful and engaging website.

## Getting Started with Design

One of the great features of WordPress.com is its nearly unlimited customization options. The possibilities of what you can do with your website are virtually endless! However, along with that flexibility comes a lot of decisions to make — from what theme to apply to the color of a specific button or link.

Recommended reading:
- If you're new to creating websites: [Website Design Best Practices and Tips for Non-Designers](https://wordpress.com/go/web-design/website-design-best-practices-and-tips-for-non-designers-infographic/)
- Why web design matters: [Why Great Web Design Matters](https://wordpress.com/go/website-building/why-great-web-design-matters/)

## Planning Your Design

Ask yourself these key questions when designing your site:

### What is the purpose of your site?
Who is your audience? Do you want a personal website, a blog, an online portfolio, a business presence, a store, or some combination? Understanding the purpose is the first step to designing a site that suits your visitors' needs.

### How should your content be organized?
You can change your site's structure at any time! Start with a design that meets your immediate needs, then expand based on what you learn. See [Site Structure Planning: Build Your Website For Success](https://wordpress.com/go/website-building/site-structure-planning/).

### Blog vs. Website?
On WordPress.com, there's really no difference between a blog and a website. They're both ways of delivering content to visitors. Main difference: blogs are updated with posts, while websites tend to have more static content. See [Do I Need a Website, a Blog, or a Website with a Blog?](https://wordpress.com/support/do-i-need-a-website-a-blog-or-a-website-with-a-blog/).

## Design Best Practices

### Draw Inspiration from Others
If you find a website with a look you admire, feel free to use it as inspiration for your own design! While content is copyrightable and should never be used without permission, no one holds a copyright on layouts, individual colors, or other design elements. See [Drawing Website Inspiration from Competitors While Remaining Ethical](https://wordpress.com/go/digital-marketing/draw-website-inspiration-from-competitors-while-remaining-ethical/).

### Use Clear Navigation
A top-level navigation menu is a great way to help visitors find content. Clear navigation menus help your site visitors get where they want to go. The choices you include and the way you structure them can shape how visitors experience your content.

### Highlight Important Content
Well-placed images and formatting help call out important information. People tend to scan for what they're looking for. The most important information, or "call-to-action," should be within the first content area of your site. Headings and subheadings are good for breaking content into digestible chunks and for SEO.

### Use Images Effectively
Images, like product pictures, logos, or art related to your content, are a great way to break up content. See [When to Use Image, Gallery, and GIF Blocks in WordPress](https://wordpress.com/go/wordpress/when-to-use-image-gallery-and-gif-blocks-in-wordpress/).

### Design for Accessibility
Consider how your site will work for users with screen readers, users who navigate with a keyboard, or users with visual impairments. Good design is accessible to the greatest number of people possible. See our [Accessibility guide](https://wordpress.com/support/accessibility/) and [10 Ways to Make Your Site More Accessible](https://wordpress.com/blog/2020/05/21/global-accessibility-awareness-day/).

### Optimize for Performance
A well-designed website should also load quickly. Visitors are more likely to stay if content displays instantly, regardless of device or connection speed. See our [Site Speed and Performance guide](https://wordpress.com/support/site-speed/).
