# Manage multiple sites

Your WordPress.com account can manage multiple websites under one login. This guide will show you how to access your sites and move your sites to other accounts.

## Multiple Sites in One Account

When you sign up for WordPress.com, you'll create your first website during the signup process. After that, you can create additional sites under the same account, although each site requires its own plan.

Each site will have at least one user (the owner), but it can have multiple users with editing access. When you create a new site, your account becomes the site's owner and administrator.

If someone invites you to administer another WordPress.com site they own, you can accept the invitation using your existing account. This lets you manage someone else's site without them having access to your other sites, unless you specifically invite them.

## Site vs. Subscription Ownership

A website can have different site and subscription owners. For example, if you create a site, you are the owner. If you invite another admin, and they purchase a plan or domain, they become the subscription owner. As the site owner, you cannot renew or cancel a subscription owned by another admin. Instead, ask the subscription owner to handle renewals or cancellations, or request that they transfer the subscription to you.

## Switching Between Sites

You can view all the sites you own and manage through the Sites list in your WordPress.com dashboard. To switch sites, access your sites list by clicking the W logo in the upper left corner of your sites' dashboards.

You can also set the Sites list to be your default landing page when you log into WordPress.com. Use the Sites page to add a new site, search, sort, and filter by privacy status (private, coming soon, public).

## Transferring Sites, Domains, and Plans

### Transfer a Site
A site's ownership, plan, and attached domains can be transferred to another administrator on that site. See our [Transfer a Site to Another WordPress.com Account](https://wordpress.com/support/transfer-a-site/) guide for step-by-step instructions.

### Transfer a Domain
To move a domain to a new owner while remaining as the overall site owner, see our [Transfer a Domain to Another WordPress.com Account](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/transfer-a-domain-to-another-wordpress-com-account/) guide.

### Transfer a Plan
To move a plan to a new owner without changing the overall site owner, the current plan owner should contact support for assistance.

## Managing Purchases Across Sites

Each site that you manage may have its own subscriptions and payments for hosting, domains, email, etc. To review payments for all of your sites:

1. Log into your WordPress.com account.
2. Visit your [Profile](https://wordpress.com/me/) or go directly to [https://wordpress.com/me/](https://wordpress.com/me/).
3. Click the "Purchases" option in the sidebar menu.

Learn more in our [Manage Your Purchases support guide](https://wordpress.com/support/manage-purchases/).

## Moving or Reusing a Plan

A paid hosting plan is applied to the site you made the purchase for. Options:

- **Start a new site with the same plan:** Register a new domain and clear the content for the current site.
- **Wrong site purchase:** Contact support for assistance.
- **Move plan to another user:** Transfer the site itself, and the plan will move with it.

## Troubleshooting Missing Sites

If your website appears to be missing from your account:

- **Wrong site:** Use the Sites Page to check other sites in your account.
- **Domain not set up:** Resolve any domain errors to get your domain working.
- **Multiple accounts:** Log out and try other email addresses.
- **Check emails:** Look for correspondence from WordPress.com (renewal notices, receipts, etc.).
- **Forgot password:** Reset via the "Lost your password?" option.
- **Not hosted with WordPress.com:** Add /wp-admin to your domain to access a WordPress.org site. Install the free Jetpack plugin to manage it in your WordPress.com account.
- **Jetpack connection error:** Resolve Jetpack connection errors to restore access.
