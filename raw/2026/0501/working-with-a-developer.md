# Working with a developer

There may be situations when you wish to hire a developer to help you work on your website. This guide shares tips you'll need to know when working with a developer for your WordPress.com site.

## What Developers Can Help With

Some examples of the types of projects that developers can help you with are:

- Third-party theme installation and customization
- Third-party plugin installation and customization
- CSS customization
- Custom PHP coding
- Custom theme and plugin development
- Site migration
- Full website development

## Adding a Developer to Your Site

If you prefer to hire a developer to modify your site, you can [invite them to be an administrator on your site](https://wordpress.com/support/invite-people/).

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never give out your own username and password to anyone, including to a developer.

The administrator role has full access to modify your site. Therefore, once your developer has finished working on your site, we suggest removing them as a user until the next time you need their assistance.

When adding the developer to your site, you can mark them as an [external collaborator](https://wordpress.com/support/invite-people/user-roles/#external-collaborator) to help keep track of whom you're adding to your site and why.

## Removing Developer Access

> **⚠️ Warning:** Once the developer completes the work, we recommend revoking their access to your site.

To remove a developer's access:

1. Visit your site's dashboard.
2. Navigate to Users → All Users.
3. Delete the user.

On sites with the Business or Commerce plan, you should also reset SFTP credentials and database access:

1. Visit your Sites list in the dashboard.
2. Click on the title of the site.
3. Navigate to the Settings tab.

![Hosting settings tab](https://en.support.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/hosting-overview-settings-tab.jpg)

4. Scroll to the SFTP/SSH section under Server.

Or, from your sites list, press ⌘K on Mac or Ctrl+K on Windows/Linux and use the Hosting Command Palette to search for "Reset SFTP/SSH password".

5. Click the "Reset password" button to reset the SFTP/SSH credentials.

Resetting the SFTP/SSH credentials will not reset your account password.

## Developer Features by Plan

If you are a freelancer or agency customizing someone else's website on WordPress.com, the following features are available depending on your client's hosting plan.

### Developer Mode
Developers can visit [https://wordpress.com/me/developer](https://wordpress.com/me/developer) and switch on the "I am a developer" toggle to opt into previews of new developer-focused features.

### WordPress.com Log In
WordPress.com websites include WordPress.com Log In to allow site access via your WordPress.com account, in addition to local users who can log in through /wp-admin.

### Free SSL Certificates
Included on all WordPress.com hosting plans: take your site from HTTP to HTTPS at no additional cost.

### Customization Options
Extend and customize WordPress to build anything you can imagine:
- Built-in CSS editor for style and layout overrides
- Install from over 50,000 plugins with automatic updates
- Upload third-party themes and create child themes

### WP-CLI & SSH
Run WP-CLI commands, automate repetitive tasks, and troubleshoot your custom code.

### Staging Sites
Test changes on a WordPress.com staging site first to identify and fix vulnerabilities before they impact your live site.

### GitHub Deployments
Speed up your development workflow by connecting your WordPress.com sites and GitHub repos. Choose from fully automatic or on-demand deployments.

### Security & Maintenance
Secure and maintain your site effortlessly with real-time backups, advanced malware scanning and removal, and continuous site monitoring.
