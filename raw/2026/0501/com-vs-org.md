# WordPress.com vs. WordPress.org

WordPress powers millions of websites, from bloggers and small businesses to massive news sites and companies. This guide will help you to understand the difference between WordPress.com and WordPress.org, and which might be the best fit for your website.

## Key Difference: Hosting

The same WordPress software powers both WordPress.com and WordPress.org sites. One of the main differences is how your website is hosted.

- **WordPress.com:** Includes managed hosting, which helps optimize your website for speed, security, and performance. Many plans with increasingly powerful features for different site types and budgets.
- **WordPress.org:** You can download the WordPress source code for free and upload it with a hosting provider of your choice ("self-hosting"). You'll need to find and pay for separate hosting and be more involved on the technical side.

## Getting Started with WordPress.com

When starting with WordPress.com, you'll sign up for an account, pick a plan that suits your needs and budget, and then create your site. WordPress.com provides managed hosting for updates, security, and backups, as well as many beautiful themes and built-in plugin functionality. The variety of plans gives you options at any level — free blog, portfolio, business website, or ecommerce store.

If you're a developer or designer creating websites for others, WordPress.com's managed hosting stack could be the perfect solution. At higher plan tiers, you'll gain access to developer-focused tools, including SFTP-SSH, WP-CLI, multi-site management, GitHub Deployments, and more.

## Getting Started with WordPress.org

To get started with WordPress.org, you'll need to download the WordPress software, find and pay for a third-party hosting service, install WordPress on your host's web server, and then build and maintain your website on your own. Each third-party hosting service will vary in their services and fees. You'll also need to research, install, and manage your own separate theme and any necessary plugins.

With self-hosted WordPress, you have great technical freedom, including the ability to make certain complex and custom code customizations and changes to the PHP environment. These types of customizations are primarily relevant for developers.

## Background

"WordPress" itself is a free and open-source content management system (CMS). WordPress.org is the home page for the WordPress project. WordPress.com is a commercial enterprise owned by Automattic, a company started by one of the founding developers of WordPress and staffed by full-time developers, designers, and support engineers.

## FAQ

### Where is my site hosted?

If you don't know where a site is hosted, or a domain is registered, enter the address into the [Site Profiler](https://wordpress.com/site-profiler) to access essential information about any site.

### Do I need separate hosting with WordPress.com?

With WordPress.com's paid plans, hosting for your site is included at no additional cost. You do not need to pay for hosting elsewhere.

### How do costs compare?

Direct cost comparisons can be difficult. Compare both price as well as features, including storage, site visits, pre-configured built-in features, security, backups, and types of support. Cheaper hosting packages tend to reflect more limitations. Beware of introductory pricing that can change after you've signed up — many hosts double or triple the price when your next payment is due. WordPress.com always offers straightforward pricing.

### Can I use plugins and custom themes?

Yes, you can install plugins and custom themes on any of the paid WordPress.com plans.

### Can I migrate my site?

Yes, you can migrate any WordPress site to or from WordPress.com, wherever it's hosted. Learn how to [migrate your site to WordPress.com](https://wordpress.com/support/import/import-an-entire-wordpress-site/) or [how to export a site from WordPress.com](https://wordpress.com/support/export/).

## Conclusion

Both WordPress.com and WordPress.org let you utilize the powerful WordPress software to build and manage a website. The main difference comes down to your specific site needs and if you'd prefer managed hosting or host your website on your own.

You can always get started for free with WordPress.com and upgrade to the plan that best suits your needs at any time. With either flavor of WordPress, you always own your own content, so you can easily migrate your website.
