# Get started with the Commerce plan

The [Commerce plan](https://wordpress.com/ecommerce/) gives you everything needed to create a profitable online store. In this guide, you will learn how to set up and customize your WooCommerce-powered store.

## Getting Started Checklist

After purchasing the [Commerce plan](https://wordpress.com/support/plan-features/ecommerce-plan/), work through a checklist to begin building your store:

1. Go to your site's dashboard.
2. Navigate to WooCommerce → Home on the left side.
3. Click each checklist item to complete the task, or click the ellipses (⋮) and select "Hide setup list" to hide the checklist.

## Customize Store Appearance

### Choose a Theme
Themes provide ready-to-use design layouts without requiring coding knowledge:
- Click "Start customizing" in the checklist, or navigate to Appearance → Themes.
- Browse themes in the [Store category](https://wordpress.com/themes/filter/store) for designs optimized for WooCommerce.

### Edit Your Store Content
- Navigate to Appearance → Editor to access the Site Editor.
- Replace sample content with your own text and images.
- Click the + block inserter to add new blocks.
- For online stores, use [WooCommerce blocks](https://woocommerce.com/document/woocommerce-blocks/) to display your products.

## Add Products

To add your first product:

1. Go to your site's dashboard.
2. Click Products in the left sidebar.
3. Review the sample products to see how they're set up.
4. Click "Add New" to create your own product.
5. For bulk imports, click Import to upload a CSV file.

### Product Types

WooCommerce supports these standard product types:
- **Simple products:** Standard items with a single price and description.
- **Variable products:** Items with options like size and color, each with its own price, stock, and SKU.
- **Virtual products:** Services or items that don't require shipping.
- **Downloadable products:** Digital files like PDFs, audio, or video.
- **Affiliate/external products:** Links to products sold on external sites.

### Commerce Plan Extras
- **Product bundles:** Group existing products together.
- **Products with custom options:** Add personalization options.
- **Products with quantity rules:** Add minimum/maximum purchase requirements.
- **Gift cards:** See the Gift Cards guide for setup instructions.

## Set Up Payments

WooCommerce Payments enables credit card, debit card, Google Pay, and Apple Pay acceptance. International buyers can also shop in their local currency.

To set up WooCommerce Payments:
1. Go to your site's dashboard.
2. Click Payments in the left sidebar.
3. Click Finish setup to complete the required information.

WooCommerce Payments uses a pay-as-you-go model and is available in 18 countries. See the [WooPayments Startup Guide](https://woocommerce.com/document/payments/).

## Configure Taxes

To enable automated tax calculations:
1. Go to WooCommerce → Settings → General.
2. Enter your complete store address.
3. Check "Enable tax rates and calculations" and save.
4. Click the Tax tab that appears.
5. Select "Enable automated taxes" and save.

For advanced tax calculations, [TaxJar](https://woocommerce.com/products/taxjar/) and [AvaTax](https://woocommerce.com/products/woocommerce-avatax/) are included with the Commerce plan.

## Set Up Shipping

A Shipping Zone is a geographical area where you deliver products:
1. Go to WooCommerce → Settings → Shipping.
2. Create zones based on your delivery regions.
3. Add shipping methods to each zone.

Default shipping methods:
- **Flat rate:** Fixed shipping cost.
- **Local pickup:** Customer collects from your location.
- **Free shipping:** No shipping cost, can be triggered by coupons or minimum order amounts.

Carrier-specific options included: UPS, USPS, Canada Post, Royal Mail, Australia Post.

## Launch Your Store

1. Go to your site's dashboard.
2. In the Getting Started Checklist, click "Launch your store".
3. Click the Launch button.

You can adjust your privacy settings at any time to make your store private temporarily.

## Manage Orders

When customers place orders, they appear in the Orders section of your dashboard. You can also create manual orders for offline sales, send invoices, or assign orders to customer accounts. Learn more about [managing WooCommerce orders](https://woocommerce.com/document/managing-orders/).

## Analytics & Marketing

WooCommerce Analytics provides insights into your store's performance. Your Commerce plan includes [WooCommerce Google Analytics](https://woocommerce.com/document/google-analytics-integration/) for detailed tracking.

Marketing tools included:
- Create coupon codes with specific conditions and restrictions.
- Sell e-gift cards to attract new customers.
- Use Google Listings & Ads to advertise across Google's network.
- Connect with social platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok.

## Extensions

Your Commerce plan includes a wide range of pre-installed extensions. For additional functionality, browse the WooCommerce marketplace for extensions that add features like subscriptions, bookings, memberships, and advanced inventory management. Explore the [curated list of extension collections](https://woocommerce.com/collections/).
