# Navigate WordPress.com

In this guide you will learn how to navigate your site's dashboard to edit your site, your sites list to manage multiple sites, and your profile to manage your account settings.

## Edit Your Site

Edit your site's content, design, and core functionality through the site's dashboard. When you visit WordPress.com and log in, you will be in your site's dashboard by default (unless you have changed your default landing page).

WordPress.com provides two different interface styles (views) to manage your WordPress site:

- **Default style:** Modern, clean interface, integrated into WordPress.com.
- **WP Admin:** Traditional WordPress interface, familiar to power users of WordPress.

Both interfaces provide access to the same features — the difference is only in how they look and are organized. Learn how to choose and switch between dashboard styles in our [admin interface style guide](https://wordpress.com/support/set-your-admin-interface-style/).

## View All Sites

If you have more than one site under your WordPress.com account, you will have a centralized dashboard where you can manage your multiple sites.

To access your dashboard, click the WordPress W icon in the upper-left corner from anywhere in WordPress.com. From here, you can:

- See all your sites.
- Access any site's dashboard or hosting overview.
- Manage domains for all sites.
- Manage themes and plugins for all sites.

You can also visit your profile, access help, or view notifications using the respective icons at the top right using the admin bar.

## Hosting Overview

Click on any site name in the Sites list to visit your site's Hosting Overview. The Hosting Overview displays key information about your site, including:

- **Visibility:** Whether your site is public, coming soon, or private.
- **Backup:** The status and date of your most recent backup (on qualified plans).
- **Performance:** Your site's current performance score based on loading speed and responsiveness.
- **Security scan:** The date of your most recent scan for malware and vulnerabilities.
- **Plan:** Your current WordPress.com plan.
- **Storage:** How much of your available storage space you've used.
- **Bandwidth:** The amount of data transferred between your site and its visitors.

## Switch Between Sites

To move from one site to another within your WordPress.com account:

1. From anywhere within your WordPress.com dashboard, click the WordPress W icon in the upper-left corner.
2. Click on the site name of the site you want to edit.
3. From your hosting overview page, click the "My Home" or "WP Admin" button in the top-right corner to access that site's dashboard.

## Set Your Default Landing Page

You can choose what you see first when you log in to WordPress.com. It's helpful to set your Hosting Dashboard or Sites list as your default landing page if you manage multiple sites.

To set your default landing page:

1. Hover over your profile icon in the top-right and select My WordPress.com account.
2. Click Account Settings.
3. In the Interface settings section, locate the Default landing page option.
4. Select the sites radio button to set your default landing page to your hosting dashboard/sites list.

Your settings will be updated automatically.

## Customize Sites List View

Switch between a grid view or table view using the Layout icon in the top-right corner of the Sites list. Or, click the Settings icon (a gear/cog) to pick which columns to display in the Sites list or Domains list views.

## Hosting Dashboard (Beta)

WordPress.com is introducing a redesigned view of the Sites list called the Hosting Dashboard. The Hosting Dashboard simplifies the operational management of your sites, including domain names, emails, backups, activity, and plans, into one place.

To opt in or out of the Hosting Dashboard:

1. Hover over your profile at the top right, and select "My WordPress.com account".
2. Select Account Settings on the left side.
3. In the "Try the new Hosting Dashboard" section, check the option to opt in, or uncheck it to return to the Sites list view.
4. Click the "Save" button.

> **Note:** Changing your Hosting Dashboard view will not change anything about how you edit your website.

## Your Profile

Your profile contains account-wide settings that affect all your sites.

In your WordPress.com account, hover over your profile icon in the top-right and select the "My WordPress.com account" button to access your profile.

- **Profile:** Edit your display name, bio, and public information.
- **Account Settings:** Manage email, password, security, and interface preferences.
- **Purchases:** View billing history and manage payments for all of your sites.
- **Email and password:** Update your login credentials.
- **Security:** Enable two-factor authentication for account protection.
- **Notifications:** Control which email updates you receive.
