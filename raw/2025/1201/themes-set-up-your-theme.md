## Open the Site Editor
You will use the [Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/) to make changes to every aspect of your site’s content and layout. In the Site Editor, you can change your site’s styles, including colors and fonts, homepage and template designs, and edit your website’s menus.
To open the Site Editor, follow these steps:
1. Visit your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home).
2. Navigate to *Appearance →* *Editor.*
Learn how to [use the Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/) before you get started.
## Edit the homepage
You most likely selected the theme you activated due to how the homepage’s design looked. You can quickly replace the sample content of the homepage with your own text and images through the Site Editor. You can also remove any unwanted content and add fresh elements using blocks or patterns.
1. Visit your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home).
2. Navigate to *Appearance →* *Editor.*
3. Click the preview on the right to open the homepage in the Site Editor.
Begin by exploring the blocks on your homepage through the [List View](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/use-list-view/) option to understand their layout and function:
List View on the Homepage Template
Here are a few of the most common blocks used to design the homepage template:
* [Query Loop block](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/query-loop-block/): Used to display the latest blog post entries. You can customize the design of the Query Loop block that automatically pulls in the contents of your posts.
* [Group block](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/group-block/): A container block that groups other blocks together into sections for you to organize and design.
* [Columns](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/columns-block/): Display content in multiple columns, with blocks added to each column.
* [Headings](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/heading-block/): Introduce new sections and organize content to help visitors (and search engines) understand the structure of your content.
* [Spacers](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/spacer-block/): Add white space between blocks and customize its height.
* …and [many more blocks](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/).
Learn more about how to [edit the default homepage](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/edit-the-default-homepage-template/).
## Create additional pages
Once you’re happy with your homepage, you can [create additional pages](https://wordpress.com/support/pages/) on your site, such as an About, Contact, or Blog page. To create a new page for your site, follow these steps:
1. Visit your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home).
2. Navigate to *Appearance →* *Editor.*
3. Click *Pages.*
4. Click the **“Add New Page”** button.
5. Give the new page a name and add your content.
6. Once you have published the new page, you can [add the page to your site’s menu](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/set-up-your-theme/#customize-the-menu).
You can also customize the design of your page templates, including the headers and footers, colors, and general layout. [Learn more about editing templates.](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/edit-a-template/)
## Customize the site’s layout
Your site’s layout is made up of several parts, including the site styles, menus, headers and footers. All of these parts come together to create templates.
### Customize the menu
With multiple pages on your site, your visitors will need a way to navigate their way around. You can choose which content appears in your site’s navigation menu and how it’s displayed, including drop-downs and different menus for mobile and desktop views.
Follow these steps to edit your site’s menu:
1. Visit your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home).
2. Navigate to *Appearance →* *Editor.*
3. Click the **“Navigation”** menu item.
You will be able to [add](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/), [remove](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/delete-a-menu/), and [reorder](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/reorder-menu-items/) the links in your menu. Learn [all about menus](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/).
### Change colors and fonts
You can change the colors and fonts for individual blocks, or change the style for your entire site.
To customize the styles for your entire site, follow these steps:
1. Visit your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home).
2. Navigate to *Appearance →* *Editor.*
3. Click the **Styles** menu item.
Learn how to [customize your site’s styles](https://wordpress.com/support/using-styles/).
### Customize the header and footer
Add your site logo, contact information, hours of operation, social media links, and more to your site’s header and footer.
The header and footer of your site are made using [template parts](https://wordpress.com/support/template-part-block/). That way, you can add the same header or footer to different templates on your site (homepage, pages, posts, etc.) and edit the header from a central location.
To edit your site’s template parts (including your site’s header and footers), follow these steps:
1. Visit your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home).
2. Navigate to *Appearance →* *Editor.*
3. Click *Patterns*, or double-click the header or footer area of the site preview.
Learn how to edit your site’s [header](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/customize-your-header/) or [footer](https://wordpress.com/support/edit-the-footer/).
