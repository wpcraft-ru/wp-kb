# Create drop-down menus
Last reviewed on April 20, 2026
Copy page
A dropdown menu, or submenu, shows additional items when you hover or click on an item in your menu. They can be useful for keeping your menu clean and organized. This guide will show you how to create a drop-down menu.

## Create a drop-down menu
Follow the steps below to create a drop-down menu.
Your site’s theme type determines which instructions to follow. Go to *Appearance* in your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/): if you see **Editor**, follow the **Site Editor** tab instructions. Otherwise, follow the **WP Admin** or **Customizer** tab instructions.
[Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-drop-down-menus/#tab-site-editor)[WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-drop-down-menus/#tab-wp-admin)[Customizer](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-drop-down-menus/#tab-customizer)
Follow these steps to add dropdown items to your site’s menu via the [**Navigation block**](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/navigation-block/):
1. From your [site’s dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/), navigate to *Appearance → Editor*.
2. Click on the right side of the screen to open the homepage in editing mode (or select **Templates** and choose a template that includes your menu).
3. Open [List View](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/use-list-view/) in the top-left corner of your screen (it’s the icon that looks like three horizontal lines above each other.)
4. Select the **Navigation block** (represented by the compass icon), which may be nested inside other blocks like **Header**, **Group**, and **Row**. Note that the block may be named “**Primary**” or another menu name.
5. In the [sidebar settings](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/customize-blocks/#block-settings), hover over a menu item and click the three dots (**⋮**) that appear.
6. Click the “**Add submenu link**” option.
7. Select the first page you’d like to appear in the dropdown.
8. Repeat steps 3-5 until you have added all desired submenu items.
9. Click the **Save** button.
Alternatively, you can create a drop-down menu using the toolbar of the [**Navigation block**](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/navigation-block/)**:**
1. Click on the menu item you wish to make into a dropdown.
2. A toolbar will appear above the menu item, where you will find the “**Add submenu**” button (the icon looks like a curved arrow with three horizontal lines next to it).
3. Click the “**Add submenu**” button in the toolbar to transform that menu item into a dropdown menu.
4. Click the **+** icon or the “Add link” option that appears to [add new links](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/) to your new dropdown menu.
5. Click **Save** to save your changes.
You can also [choose how your drop-down menus open](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/customize-the-menu-design/#choose-how-drop-down-menus-open) — on hover or on click.
On sites with [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes), you can add drop-down menus using [WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/dashboard/) using the following steps:
1. Visit your dashboard.
2. Go to *Appearance → Menus.*
3. Ensure the correct menu is selected at the top of the screen.
4. Click and drag a menu item to the right to “nest” them under the item just above it.
5. Click the **“Save Menu”** button to save your changes.
You can undo this by dragging them to the left again. Click the “**Save Menu**” button when you’re done.
On sites with [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes), you can create drop-down menus using the [Customizer](https://wordpress.com/support/customizer/) by following these steps:
1. [Add all the menu items](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/) you wish to have in your site’s menu.
2. Click on the “**Reorder”** link that appears below your menu items.
3. Click the **>** right arrow to nest a page underneath the one above, creating a drop-down item.
4. Once you’re happy with your drop-down menu, click the “**Done**” button.
5. Click the “**Save Changes**” button to publish your changes to the site.

## Create drop-down menus with the AI assistant
> This section applies to sites created with the [AI Website Builder](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/) (all paid plans) or sites that have [enabled the AI assistant](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/ai-tools/) (Business and Commerce plans). Open the AI assistant by clicking the AI icon (a four-pointed star) at the bottom of the screen.
The AI assistant can create dropdown menus and nested navigation directly on your site. Here are some things you can ask it to do:
* “Make Training a dropdown with three submenu items: Skills, Film Room, and Conditioning.”
* “Move the Contact menu item into a submenu under About.”
* “Add a submenu under Schedule with items: Clinics, Camps, and 1-on-1 Sessions.”
* “Set my drop-down menus to open on click instead of hover.”
## Add a menu item without a link
You can create a “non-clickable” menu item, which is useful when creating dropdown menus if you want the top-level menu item to include submenu items without the top-level item leading to any page. The trick is to use a # symbol as the menu item’s link. The item can still be clicked but will not navigate to a new page. Follow these steps to create a “non-clickable” menu item.
Your site’s theme type determines which instructions to follow. Go to *Appearance* in your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/): if you see **Editor**, follow the **Site Editor** tab instructions. Otherwise, follow the **WP Admin** or **Customizer** tab instructions.
[Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-drop-down-menus/#tab-site-editor)[WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-drop-down-menus/#tab-wp-admin)[Customizer](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-drop-down-menus/#tab-customizer)
Follow these steps to create an unclickable menu item:
1. From your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/), navigate to *Appearance → Editor*.
2. Select **Templates** and choose a template that includes your menu, such as Home or Front Page.
3. Open [List View](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/use-list-view/) in the top-left corner of your screen (it’s the icon that looks like three horizontal lines above each other).
4. Select the **[Navigation block](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/navigation-block/)** (it may be nested inside other blocks like **Header**, **Group**, or **Row**).
5. Click the **+ block inserter** to [add a new menu item.](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/)
6. In the window that appears, click “**Add block**” and select the “**Custom Link**” option.
7. Type a # symbol and press Enter or Return on your keyboard.
8. Click the pencil icon to edit the new menu item. Write what you want the menu item to say in the “**Text**” field. Leave only the # symbol in the “**Link**” field to make it an unclickable link.
9. Click the “**Save**” button to submit the change.
To create a menu item without a link, add a # symbol in the URL field.
You can also edit an existing menu link to make it unclickable. Click on the existing menu item, and a toolbar will appear above the menu item, where you will find the **Link** button (the icon looks like a literal link from a chain.) Edit the menu item and replace the current link with the # symbol. The GIF below demonstrates the process:
On sites with [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes), you can add an unclickable link to your menu using [WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/dashboard/) by following these steps:
1. Go to *Appearance → Menus.*
2. Choose your menu.
3. Click the “**Custom Links**” section under the **“Add menu items”** header.
4. In the *URL* field enter the # symbol, as shown in the image on the right.
5. In the *Link Text* field enter the text you want to appear in your menu, as shown in the image on the right.
6. Click the “**Add to Menu**” button.
7. Click the “**Save Menu**” button.
On sites with [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes), you can add an unclickable link to your menu using the [Customizer](https://wordpress.com/support/customizer/) by following these steps:
1. Go to *Appearance → Customize → Menus.*
2. Choose your menu.
3. Click the “**Add Items**” button.
4. Click the **Custom Links** option.
5. In the *URL* field enter the # symbol, as shown in the image on the right.
6. In the *Link Text* field enter the text you want to appear in your menu, as shown in the image on the right.
7. Click the “**Add to Menu**” button.
8. Click the “**Save Changes**” button.
