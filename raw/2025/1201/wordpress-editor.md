## Access the WordPress Editor
You can access the WordPress Editor in different ways depending on what you want to edit.
### Edit the website design
To edit your website’s structure, navigate to *Appearance → Editor* in your [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home). From here, you can edit:
* [Menus](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/), [header](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/customize-your-header/), and [footer](https://wordpress.com/support/edit-the-footer/).
* [Site templates](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/), which control the layout used by pages and posts.
* [Patterns](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/create-a-pattern/), which are reusable content you can add across multiple parts of your site.
Changes made here affect your site’s design and templates globally — not the content of individual pages or posts. To edit the text, images, or other content on a specific page, see [Edit individual pages](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/#edit-individual-pages) or [Edit individual posts](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/#edit-individual-posts) below.
💡
> If you don’t see *Appearance → Editor*, your theme does not support the Site Editor. Consider switching to a [supported theme](https://wordpress.com/themes/filter/full-site-editing/) to access the latest editing features.
### Edit individual pages
To create or edit a page from your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/), follow these steps:
1. Click on **Pages** in the menu on the left.
2. Click the **“Add Page”** button to create a new page, or click the title of an existing page to edit it.
Learn how to [create, customize, and publish your site’s pages](https://wordpress.com/support/pages/).
You can also create or edit pages through the [Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/) by following these steps:
1. From your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/), navigate to *Appearance → Editor*.
2. Select **Pages** on the left side.
3. Click the **“Add Page”** button to create a new page, or hover over a page and select the **Edit** option to the left of the page title.
### Edit individual posts
To write new [posts](https://wordpress.com/support/posts/) or edit existing posts, follow these steps:
1. From your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/), click on **Posts**.
2. Click the **“Add Post”** button to create a new post, or click the title of an existing post to edit it.
Learn how to [create, publish, and share your posts](https://wordpress.com/support/posts/).
## Write and design with the AI assistant
> This section applies to sites created with the [AI Website Builder](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/) (all paid plans) or sites that have [enabled the AI assistant](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/ai-tools/) (Business and Commerce plans). Open the AI assistant by clicking the AI icon (a four-pointed star) at the bottom of the screen.
The AI assistant can help you write and design content directly in the editor. Here are some things you can ask it to do:
* “Write an introduction paragraph for my About page.”
* “Generate a three-column features section for my homepage.”
* “Improve the readability of this paragraph: [paste text].”
* “Suggest a layout for a product landing page.”
## Build pages with AI agents
> You can use AI agents like Claude, Cursor, ChatGPT, OpenClaw, and more to read and edit your website securely. [Enable MCP tool access for AI agents](https://wordpress.com/support/model-context-protocol-mcp-settings/).
AI agents can create and update pages and posts on your site directly. Here are some things you can ask an agent to do:
* “Add a hero section to my homepage with a welcome message and a Get Started button.”
* “Create a new About page with three columns describing my services.”
* “Update the headline on my homepage to ‘Welcome to [site name].'”
* “Add a contact form to my Contact page.”
