## Add links to your menu
Make your new content easy to find by adding links to your navigation menu. This guide shows you how, with additional design options available for Site Editor themes.
Your site’s theme type determines which instructions to follow. Go to *Appearance* in your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/): if you see **Editor**, follow the **Site Editor** tab instructions. Otherwise, follow the **WP Admin** or **Customizer** tab instructions.
[Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#tab-site-editor)[WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#tab-wp-admin)[Customizer](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#tab-customizer)
In the [Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/), your menu is created using the [Navigation block](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/navigation-block/). Follow these steps to add a new menu item:
1. From your [site’s dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/), navigate to *Appearance → Editor*.
2. Click on the right side of the screen to open the homepage in editing mode (or select **Templates** and choose a template that includes your menu).
3. Open [List View](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/use-list-view/) in the top-left corner of your screen (it’s the icon that looks like three horizontal lines above each other.)
4. Select the **Navigation block** (represented by the compass icon), which may be nested inside other blocks like **Header**, **Group**, and **Row**. Note that the block may be named “**Primary**” or another menu name.
5. Click the **+ block inserter** icon that now appears on the **Navigation block**.
6. A pop-up will appear for you to add a new link. Choose from one of the following options:
* Choose from any of your recently updated pages.
* Search by name and select any published [page](https://wordpress.com/support/pages/), [post](https://wordpress.com/support/posts/), [category](https://wordpress.com/support/posts/categories/), or [tag](https://wordpress.com/support/posts/tags/). Private or draft content will not appear here.
* Manually type or paste to add any link (including an external one from a different site) to your menu.
* Create a new page.
* Click the “**+ add block**” link to [add blocks to your menu](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#add-menu-items).
7. Click the **Save** button to publish your changes.
Now that you have added the menu items, you can [reorder the items](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/reorder-menu-items/), [create dropdown menus](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-drop-down-menus/), and do much more. [Take a look at our menus documentation](https://wordpress.com/support/category/content-and-media/menus/) to learn what you can do.
**Video tutorial**
On sites with [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes), you can add menu items using [WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/dashboard/) by following these steps:
1. Go to *Appearance → Menus* in your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/).
2. Ensure the correct menu is selected at the top of the screen:
1. Under *Add menu items,* tick the boxes for each of the new menu items you wish to add. You can choose from Pages, Posts, Categories, Custom Links, and [other types of menu items](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#types-of-menu-items).
2. Click the “**Add to Menu**” button. Each of the menu items you selected in the previous step will be added to the menu.
3. If you wish to change the order the menu items appear in, click and drag each one to the desired spot. [More on reordering menus.](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/reorder-menu-items/)
4. Click the “**Save Menu**” button at the bottom of the screen when you’re done:
On sites with [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes), you can add menu items using the Customizer by following these steps:
1. Go to Appearance → *Customizer* in your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/).
2. Click the **Menus** option.
3. Choose the menu you wish to add to. If there is no menu here, [create a menu first](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-a-menu/).
4. Click the “**+ Add Items**” button.
5. Under *Pages*, you will see a list of your site’s published pages (private pages and draft pages will not appear in the list.) Click on any page title to add it to your menu. You may also add posts, portfolio projects, blog categories, custom links to other websites, and [other types of menu items](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#types-of-menu-items).
6. If you wish to rearrange the items or make dropdown menus, click the “**Reorder**” link. [Learn more about how to reorder menu items.](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/reorder-menu-items/)
7. Click the “**Save Changes**“ button to publish the changes to your site.

**Add New Page**
When adding pages to your menu, you’ll see a list of your published pages followed by an “**Add New Page**“option that looks like this:
The “**Add New Page**“option will create a brand new blank page on your site and add that page to your menu. You can later add content to this page by going to *Pages* in your dashboard. Posts, projects, and testimonials have similar options.
If you have already created a page and wish to add it to your menu, do not use the “**Add New Page**“option. Instead, select the existing page from the list above. If your page is not listed there, it could be because:
* You have not published the page yet. Exit the Customize area and click on *Pages*. Check if the page is in *Drafts* or *Scheduled*. Remember, you can only add a page to your menu if it is published.
* You may have created a post or project, instead of a page.
## Add menu items
If you are using a Site Editor theme, you can add buttons, social links, a subscribe button, and more to your site’s menu. Follow these steps to add menu items to your navigation:
1. From your [site’s dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/), navigate to *Appearance → Editor*.
2. Select **Templates** and choose a template that includes your menu, such as Home or Front Page.
3. Open [List View](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/use-list-view/) in the top-left corner of your screen (it’s the icon that looks like three horizontal lines above each other.)
4. Select the **Navigation block**, which may be nested inside other blocks like **Header**, **Group**, and **Row**.
5. Click the “**+ Block Inserter**” icon that now appears on the **Navigation block**.
6. Click the “**+ Add block**” option in the pop-up that appears:
* Your most recently used blocks will appear, so you can quickly select a block to add.
* Click the **browse all** button to choose from the [design, widget, or theme blocks](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#types-of-menu-items).
7. Click **Save** to save your changes.
## Add menu items with the AI assistant
> This section applies to sites created with the [AI Website Builder](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/) (all paid plans) or sites that have [enabled the AI assistant](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/ai-tools/) (Business and Commerce plans). Open the AI assistant by clicking the AI icon (a four-pointed star) at the bottom of the screen.
The AI assistant can add pages, external links, and email addresses to your menus directly. Here are some things you can ask it to do:
* “Add a new menu item called Blog that links to /blog in the main navigation.”
* “Create a menu item called Instagram that links to [https://instagram.com/%5Byourhandle%5D.”](https://instagram.com/%5Byourhandle%5D.%26#8221);
* “Link the Contact menu item to my email address instead of the contact page.”
* “Add a Privacy Policy link in the footer menu that points to /privacy-policy.”
## Types of menu items
Here is a list of each type of content you can add to your menu:
* **Home link**: a link to your site’s home page.
* **Pages**: a link to a page on your site.
* **Posts**: a blog post on your site.
+ **Categories**: a page of blog posts from a given [category](https://wordpress.com/support/posts/categories/).
+ **Tags**: a page of blog posts with a given [tag](https://wordpress.com/support/posts/tags/).
* **Custom Link**: a URL linking to another website.
* **Testimonials**: a link to [testimonials](https://wordpress.com/support/testimonials/).
* **Projects**: a link to a page of your [portfolio projects](https://wordpress.com/support/portfolios/).
+ **Project Types**: a link to a specific portfolio project type.
+ **Project Tags**: a link to a specific portfolio project tag.
* **Blocks**: Choose from design, widget, or theme blocks (via the [Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#add-menu-items) method only), including:
+ [Buttons](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/buttons-block/)
+ [Subscriber login](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/subscriber-login-block/)
+ [Subscribe](https://wordpress.com/support/tag/subscribe/)
+ [Social Icons](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/social-icons-block/)
+ [Submenu](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-drop-down-menus/)
+ …and more
Some [WordPress plugins](https://wordpress.com/support/plugins/) add custom menu item types that appear alongside your standard options.
