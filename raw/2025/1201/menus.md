## About menus
A menu (also called navigation) makes it easy for your visitors to find their way around your website’s pages and other content. A menu is commonly displayed prominently in the site’s header or sidebar.
The menu displays links to your site’s pages. It’s generally recommended to keep your list of pages short to avoid overwhelming the user. If you have several pages to feature in your menu, you can use submenus (also called dropdowns) to keep your navigation links neat and organized.
## Where to edit your menu
The process for editing your menu will depend on your [WordPress theme](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/).
To find your menu settings, visit your [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home) and check under *Appearance* on the left side. Look for one of the following options:
* **Editor**: Edit your menu here on modern WordPress themes [using the Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/).
* **Customize** or **Menus**: You’ll find menu options here on classic WordPress themes.

## Update menus with the AI assistant
> This section applies to sites created with the [AI Website Builder](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/) (all paid plans) or sites that have [enabled the AI assistant](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/ai-tools/) (Business and Commerce plans). Open the AI assistant by clicking the AI icon (a four-pointed star) at the bottom of the screen.
The AI assistant can make changes to your site’s menus directly — adding, removing, and rearranging items. Here are some things you can ask it to do:
* “Add a new menu item called Blog that links to /blog in the main navigation.”
* “Rename the Training menu item to Workouts.”
* “Add a Privacy Policy link in the footer menu that points to /privacy-policy.”
* “Check my menus for duplicate items and remove the duplicates.”
