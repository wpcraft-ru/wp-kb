## About themes
A theme is your site’s visual design and overall layout. It controls how your website’s content is presented to a visitor. Themes include design components like color schemes, typography, page layouts, and other elements that create a consistent and cohesive appearance throughout the website.
Themes provide a starting point for your website’s look and functionality. Instead of building your website’s design from scratch, a theme provides the design framework you need to launch your website quickly. With a theme, you can start creating your content immediately without spending too much time deciding on how the content looks. A well-designed theme does the hard work for you!
You can also [change from one theme to another](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/changing-themes/) without losing your website’s content, applying the new changes automatically.
Theme: [Twenty Twenty-Four](https://wordpress.com/theme/twentytwentyfour/)
## How to choose a theme
With so many options, it can be helpful to think about what you’re looking for in a theme. The main thing to remember is that you are in complete control over your site’s layout. After activating a WordPress.com theme, you can [use the Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/) to change the text, images, colors, fonts, layout, and position of all the elements on your site.
Some factors to consider when deciding on a theme include:
* **Do you like the look of the header area?** You’ll typically see the site title, menu, and logo here.
* **Do you like how the content is organized and presented?** While you can change how this looks, choosing a layout you like can mean less work moving things around later.
* **Do you like how the theme appears on mobile and tablet screens?** To see this, open the live demo and use the selector at the top to switch between desktop, tablet, and mobile. All WordPress.com themes are responsive to all screen sizes, including mobile phones.
Theme: [Gourmetto](https://wordpress.com/theme/gourmetto)
When looking at themes, focus on the overall layout rather than the subject matter shown in the demo. A theme’s demo site may showcase an example of a furniture store, a music portfolio, a fashion site, or a food blog. However, your site can be customized for any topic imaginable once you swap out the demo text and images for your own.
Theme: [Iotix](https://wordpress.com/theme/iotix)
## Browse the available themes
To browse the collection of themes available on WordPress.com, visit **Appearance* → *[Themes](https://wordpress.com/themes/)* (or *Appearance* → *Theme Showcase** if using [WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/set-your-admin-interface-style/)) in your WordPress.com dashboard. These themes don’t need to be uploaded or installed on your site — they can be activated with the click of a button.
A range of themes is available to suit all types of websites and budgets, with new themes added all the time. As you browse the themes, you’ll notice one of the following labels right below the theme’s name:
* **Free**: Themes that are available to all WordPress.com sites.
* **Upgrade**: Some themes are available on paid plans sites.
* **Partner**: [Partner themes](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/partner-themes/) are available to sites on our [Business and Commerce plans](https://wordpress.com/plans/). These themes are created by third-party theme developers specifically for WordPress.com and require an annual or monthly subscription.
* **Community**: Community themes are available to sites on our [Business and Commerce plans](https://wordpress.com/plans/). These themes are created by [third-party theme developers](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#third-party-themes) unaffiliated with WordPress.com. Popular themes such as [Astra](https://wordpress.com/theme/astra/), [OceanWP](https://wordpress.com/theme/oceanwp/), and many more can be found here to install on your WordPress.com website.
## Learn more about themes
Our dedicated theme guides will help you activate, set up, and customize themes on your site:
## [Activate a theme](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/choose-a-theme/)
Choosing a theme is an important step at the beginning of creating your website. This guide will show you how to find themes and choose one that fits the vision you have for your site.
## [Set up your theme](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/set-up-your-theme/)
After choosing a theme, your next step will be to set up your theme to look exactly how you want. If you’re not sure how to get started, follow the steps on this page.
## [Change your theme](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/changing-themes/)
You can change the theme of your site to give your existing content a fresh look. This guide explains how to switch to a new theme.
## [Upload a theme](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/uploading-setting-up-custom-themes/)
This guide will show you how to upload any theme to your WordPress.com site.
## [Create a child theme](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/child-themes/)
If you have knowledge of HTML and PHP, you can modify your theme’s underlying code by creating a child theme.
## [Delete a theme](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/delete-a-theme/)
This guide will show you how to remove themes from your site.
## [Different types of themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/)
In this guide, you will learn about the different types of themes you can use for your site.
## [Retired themes](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/retired-themes/)
This guide explains what it means when a theme has been retired and what to do about it.
## [Partner themes](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/partner-themes/)
Partner themes are developed by third-party creators who have made their themes available to purchase and install on WordPress.com.
## [Edit theme files directly](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/edit-theme-files/)
In this guide, learn how to edit your theme’s files.
