# Add content using blocks
Last reviewed on March 9, 2026
Copy page
A block is a single piece of content in the [WordPress Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/). Think of them as the literal building blocks of your website. You can add blocks for text, images, video, audio, columns, spaces, page breaks, payment buttons, calendars, and [much more!](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/#blocks) This guide will teach you how to get started with adding blocks.

## How to add a block
You can add a block anywhere you see the + button (called the **Block Inserter)**. This will appear in several places, including:
1. Click the **+ Block Inserter** in the top-left corner of the editor. This option is great for previewing different block styles:
Preview a block before you add it by hovering over the block icon.
1. On a new line (created by pressing Enter/Return on your keyboard), click the **+ Block Inserter** at the side of an empty block:
1. In the toolbar that appears for a block you have clicked on, select the … (three dots) and choose *Add before* or *Add after* to insert a new block above or below the current block:
**Pro Tip:** Once you are familiar with the names of different blocks, you can quickly add a block by typing a forward slash followed by the block name. For example: `/image` or `/heading`.
Shortcut to add a block
To remove a block, see [Remove a block](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/remove-a-block/).
## Add a block in a specific position
If you already have content on your page and would like to add new blocks at a specific place in that content, you can do it in one of two ways:
### Option 1: Use List View
**[List View](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/use-list-view/)** is an essential tool when working on your site’s content. To access **List View** and add new blocks, follow these steps:
1. Click the three horizontal lines icon in the top left corner of the editor to open **List View**.
2. Click on the existing block you want to add the new block next to.
3. Click the three dots on the right side of the now-highlighted block.
4. Choose **Add before** or **Add after**.
5. Click on the **+ Block Inserter**.
6. Search for the block you’d like to add and click it to insert it in that spot.
### Option 2: Use the cursor
Using your computer’s mouse/touchpad, you can also add new blocks in certain spots by following these steps:
1. Point your cursor to the spot on the page where you want to add a new block, and hover in that spot.
2. Click on the **+ Block Inserter** that appears in about a second.
3. Search for the block you’d like to add and click it to insert it in that spot.
## Exploring blocks
There’s a block for every possible element you might want to add to your site. To browse the full list of blocks, click the **+ Block Inserter** in the upper-left corner of the editor. View a [full list of blocks and their guides here](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/).
Here, you will see all the blocks divided into categories. When you hover over a block, it will show you a preview with additional information.
Here is also where you can access [block patterns](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/block-pattern/) (groups of blocks in useful designs). Take a look at our [library of patterns](https://wordpress.com/patterns?ref=support-docs) for a vast collection of professionally designed patterns that you can use to build your website.
If you have [installed plugins](https://wordpress.com/support/plugins/), you may see extra blocks here added by your plugins.
Adding blocks and block patterns from the Block Inserter.
## Add content with the AI assistant
> This section applies to sites created with the [AI Website Builder](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/) (all paid plans) or sites that have [enabled the AI assistant](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/ai-tools/) (Business and Commerce plans). Open the AI assistant by clicking the AI icon (a four-pointed star) at the bottom of the screen.
The AI assistant on WordPress.com can help you write and generate content on your site. Here are some sample prompts you can copy and use:
* “Write a short introduction paragraph for a page about [your topic].”
* “Write a call-to-action paragraph encouraging visitors to get in touch.”
* “Generate a bulleted list of five services my business offers.”
* “Write a brief team bio for my About page.”
## Add content with AI agents
> You can use AI agents like Claude, Cursor, ChatGPT, OpenClaw, and more to read and edit your website securely. [Enable MCP tool access for AI agents](https://wordpress.com/support/model-context-protocol-mcp-settings/).
AI agents can add, arrange, and populate blocks across your pages and posts from a natural language prompt. Here are some prompts to try:
* “Add an Image block followed by a paragraph to the top of my About page.”
* “Insert a Button block labeled “Learn More” that links to my Services page.”
* “Add a Separator block between each section on my homepage.”
* “Insert a Quote block after the second paragraph of my latest post.”
## See your most used blocks
If you find yourself adding the same few blocks all the time, you can surface your most-used blocks at the top of the list of blocks using the following steps:
1. Click the ellipses icon (the three dots) in the top-right corner of the editor screen.
2. Choose *Preferences* → *Blocks*.
3. Toggle on *Show most used blocks.*
This makes it easier to select from the most used blocks on your site.
