# Use the Site Editor
Last reviewed on April 24, 2026

Using the Site Editor, you can visually edit your entire site—including headers, footers, and templates—no code required. In this guide, you will learn about the core tools built into the Site Editor.
If you’re new to block themes, [learn how themes, templates, and pages](https://wordpress.com/support/themes/understand-themes/) fit together.

## Access the Site Editor
The Site Editor is what you will use to modify your website’s design, including templates, navigation menus, and pages.
To visit the Site Editor, follow these steps:
1. Start from your [site’s dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/).
2. Hover over, or click *Appearance* on the left side.
3. Click *Editor*.
If you do not see *Editor* under *Appearance*, your theme does not support the Site Editor. Consider switching to a [Block theme to access the Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#block-themes-recommended).
## Navigate the Site Editor
When you arrive at the Site Editor, you will find the **Design** panel on the left, with the following options:
* [Styles](https://wordpress.com/support/using-styles/): Customize colors, fonts, and other design aspects of your site.
* [Navigation](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/): Manage your site’s navigation menus.
* Identity: Manage your [site icon](https://wordpress.com/support/site-icons/) and [site logo](https://wordpress.com/support/site-logo/).
* [Pages](https://wordpress.com/support/pages/): Edit the content of individual pages of your site.
* [Templates](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/): Edit the structural layout of your site’s content.
* [Patterns](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/template-part-block/): Edit smaller parts of your templates (such as the header and footer) and [patterns you’ve created.](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/create-a-pattern/)
On the right, you’ll find a preview of your website’s homepage. Click anywhere in this area to close the left sidebar and open your homepage in the editor.
## Use the Site Editor
Click the preview on the right of the Site Editor to open your homepage in the site editing tools. You will see the same editing tools when you edit a specific page, template, or pattern.
### Top menu
At the top of the Site Editor are several options, explained below, from left to right
1. **Open Navigation**: Click once on your site icon (or the default W logo if you haven’t set one yet) to return to the left sidebar options described in the previous section. Click it again to return to your [site’s dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home).
2. **Block Inserter**: Click this to [add a new block.](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/add-content-blocks/)
3. **Undo:** Reverse the most recent action taken.
4. **Redo:** Un-reverse an action you reversed using the Undo button.
5. **List View**: View a list of all the blocks that are on the page. [List View](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/use-list-view/) is useful for seeing your page’s structure and editing, moving, and removing blocks.
6. **Command Center:** This shows the name of the [template you are editing](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/). Click this to search through your site’s templates.
7. **View**: Change the viewport to view your content on desktop, tablet, or mobile screens. Click “**View site**” to see the result of your changes.
8. **Help Center**: Search our helpful guides or contact support on eligible plans.
9. **Styles:** Customize the colors, fonts, and more. [Learn more about Styles.](https://wordpress.com/support/using-styles/)
10. **Settings**: Display or hide the [settings sidebar](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/#settings-sidebar-right) on the right.
11. **Save:** Save your changes. [Learn more about saving](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/#save-changes).
12. **More Options:** Opens [additional settings and tools](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/learn-the-interface/#more-options).
### Styles
Use **Styles** in the Site Editor to choose color palettes, fonts, layouts, and more design aspects that affect your site as a whole. [Learn how to use Styles.](https://wordpress.com/support/using-styles/)
### Settings sidebar (right)
The Settings sidebar includes information about the [template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/) you are editing and options for customizing specific blocks within the template.
The Settings sidebar appears on the right side of the editor.
If you do not have the sidebar on the right, click the Settings icon in the top-right corner to bring up the settings. This icon looks like a square with two uneven columns:
You will see the name of the [template you are working on](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/) at the top. Click the ellipses (three dots) to the right of the template name to [reset the template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/reset-a-template/) to the theme’s default, undoing any customizations you made.
If any previous versions of the template exist, you will see a **Revisions** link below the template description. Use this to restore a previous version of your customized template. [Learn more about template revisions.](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/edit-a-template/#restore-a-template-revision)
Under the **Content** section, you’ll find [template parts](https://wordpress.com/support/template-part-block/) (commonly the header and footer), which you can click to update those site-wide elements.
Click on the **Block** tab at the top to view the [settings for the specific block you have selected](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/customize-blocks/).
### Save changes
When you click the **Save** button to save changes you’ve made in the Site Editor, you will see an additional screen that displays the changes that will be saved.
By default, the boxes next to each change are checked. You can uncheck a change to choose not to save it.
When you save changes to a template, template part, or navigation menu, those changes will apply everywhere that template or menu is used on your site.
## Edit your site with the AI assistant
> This section applies to sites created with the [AI Website Builder](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/) (all paid plans) or sites that have [enabled the AI assistant](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/ai-tools/) (Business and Commerce plans). Open the AI assistant by clicking the AI icon (a four-pointed star) at the bottom of the screen.
The AI assistant on WordPress.com can be a partner at every stage of site editing — from choosing a color palette to planning your navigation and templates. Here are some sample prompts you can copy and use:
* “Suggest a color palette and font pairing for a modern food and recipe blog.”
* “How should I structure the navigation for a freelance portfolio site with a blog and contact page?”
* “What header and footer layout would suit a minimalist small business site?”
* “I want my homepage to feel welcoming and professional — what layout would you recommend?”
## Additional guides
Here are more helpful resources and tutorials to learn more about all aspects of site editing:
### Get started with the editor
The following guides cover the basics of **block editing**. Each piece of content you add to your site is contained within a **block**. The guides below will help you become comfortable with editing, adding, moving, and removing blocks.
## [WordPress Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/)
The WordPress editor uses a system of blocks to create the pages and posts on your website. This guide explains how you can use blocks to create beautiful layouts. With blocks, the possibilities are endless!
## [Add content using blocks](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/add-content-blocks/)
A block is a single piece of content in the WordPress Editor. This guide will teach you how to add blocks to your site.
## [Select and move blocks](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/select-and-move-blocks/)
This guide will show you how to move blocks to a different part of the page.
## [Remove a block](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/remove-a-block/)
This guide will show you how to remove blocks from a page.
### Design your site in the editor
In the Site Editor, you can design your site’s templates, including the headers, footers, and content areas. The following guides will introduce you to the basic concepts of designing your site in the Site Editor.
## [Edit the header](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/customize-your-header/)
This guide will show you how to customize your website’s header, where site name, logo, and menu typically appear.
## [Create a sidebar](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/add-a-sidebar/)
Learn how to add a sidebar to any page or template.
## [Edit the post layout](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/edit-the-single-template/)
In this guide, learn how to modify the layout of your blog posts.
## [Hide page titles using the Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/hide-page-or-post-title/)
Learn how to hide page titles from specific templates or individual pages or posts in this guide.
## [Edit the footer](https://wordpress.com/support/edit-the-footer/)
This guide will show you how to edit the footer on your website.
