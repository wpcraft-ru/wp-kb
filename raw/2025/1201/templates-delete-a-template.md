# Delete a template
Last reviewed on April 10, 2026
Copy page
You can delete any [template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/) you created on your WordPress.com site. You cannot delete the default templates that came with your theme, but you can [reset them to their original design](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/reset-a-template/) instead.

## Delete a custom template
To delete a single custom template, follow these steps:
1. Visit your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/).
2. Navigate to *Appearance → Editor*.
3. In the left sidebar, click the **Templates** option.
4. Find the custom template you want to delete and click the ellipsis (three dots) at the top right of the template thumbnail.
5. Click **Delete** in the menu.
6. Click **Delete** in the confirmation window. Your custom template is now removed from your site.
If you are already editing a template, click the site logo at the top-left of your screen to open the sidebar. From there, click the ellipsis (three dots) next to the template name to find the **Delete** option.
## Delete multiple custom templates
To delete several custom templates at once, follow these steps:
1. Visit your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/).
2. Navigate to *Appearance → Editor*.
3. In the left sidebar, click the **Templates** option.
4. Select the custom templates you want to delete. To select templates individually, hover over a template thumbnail and click the checkbox that appears on it. To select all custom templates at once, click the checkbox in the bar at the bottom of the screen.
1. In the bottom bar, click **Delete**. (If **Delete** does not appear, none of the selected templates are custom templates you created.)
2. Click **Delete** in the confirmation window. The selected custom templates are now removed from your site.
## Default templates can’t be deleted
WordPress.com does not allow you to delete the default templates that came with your theme. If you changed a default template and want to remove those changes, [reset the template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/reset-a-template/) to return it to its original design. If you want a different template applied to a specific page or post, [change the template that page or post uses](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/apply-a-template/).
You can delete any template you created yourself. To see which templates on your site are custom and which came with your theme, open the [Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/) and go to *[Templates](https://wordpress.com/switch-site?route=%2Fwp-admin%2Fsite-editor.php?p=%2Ftemplate)*. Templates you created show your name, and templates from your theme show the theme name:
## Related tasks
* **[Reset a template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/reset-a-template/)**: If you want to undo changes to a default template, reset it instead of deleting it.
* **[Apply a template to a page](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/apply-a-template/)**: If you want a different template on a specific page or post, change which template it uses.
* **[Create a template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/create-a-template/)**: If you need a new custom template for your site, create one from the Site Editor, the page editor, or by duplicating an existing template.
* **[Edit a template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/edit-a-template/)**: If you want to change a template’s layout rather than delete it, edit it instead.
* **[Templates](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/)**: For an overview of how templates work and the common types of templates on your site.
