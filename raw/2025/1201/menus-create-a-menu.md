## Create a menu
Your site’s theme type determines which instructions to follow. Go to *Appearance* in your site’s [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/): if you see **Editor**, follow the **Site Editor** tab instructions. Otherwise, follow the **WP Admin** or **Customizer** tab instructions.
[Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-a-menu/#tab-site-editor)[WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-a-menu/#tab-wp-admin)[Customizer](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-a-menu/#tab-customizer)
Your menu comes from the [Navigation block](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/navigation-block/) in the [Site Editor](https://wordpress.com/support/site-editor/). To create a new menu, follow these steps.
1. Visit your site’s dashboard.
2. Navigate to *Appearance →* *Editor.*
3. Select the [template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/) or [template part](https://wordpress.com/support/template-part-block/) you want the navigation menu to appear, or click on the site preview on the right side of the screen to open the homepage editor.
4. Click the **+ block Inserter** icon.
5. Use the search field in the block inserter to search for “navigation.”
6. Click the **Navigation block** to add the block to your site.
[Learn how to customize the Navigation block](https://wordpress.com/support/wordpress-editor/blocks/navigation-block/).
**Video Tutorial – Create a Menu**
Slow down or speed up the video using the controls in the lower-right corner after clicking the “Play” icon.
On sites with [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes), you can add menu items using [WP Admin](https://wordpress.com/support/dashboard/) by following these steps:
1. Visit your [dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home).
2. Go to *Appearance → Menus*.
3. Click the “**create a new menu**” link, and give your menu a name.
4. Under **“Menu** **Settings,”** select at least one *Display location*. The locations you see here will vary depending on your theme.
5. Optionally, you can select *Auto add pages* so new pages will be added automatically when you create them.
6. Click the “**Create Menu**” button to begin [adding items to your new menu](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#tab-customizer).
If you notice that your *Appearance* menu has both **Editor** and **Customizer**, you may be using a [Universal theme](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/). The preferred method for editing menu items in Universal themes is to use the [**Site Editor** design interface](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/create-a-menu/#create-a-new-menu).
On sites with [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes), you can add menu items using the Customizer by following these steps:
1. Go to *Appearance* → [*Customize*](https://wordpress.com/customize/).
2. Click the “**Menus**” option.
1. You will see that a menu has been created for you by default, usually called something like *Primary* or *Main Menu*. [Jump to the next guide](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#tab-customizer) to start adding links to this menu.
If you don’t see a menu here already, click the “**Create New Menu**” button.
1. Give your menu a name, such as “Primary Menu”. The name is not shown publicly.
2. Under **Menu Locations**, check at least one box. Your menu will appear in the location you choose. The locations you see here will vary depending on your theme.
3. Click the “**Next**” button to begin [adding items to your new menu](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/#tab-customizer).
**Menu Locations**
The Menu Locations setting in the Customizer determines where on your site your menu appears. The options you see here will be different depending on your site’s theme.
Make sure to check at least one box under *Menu Locations*. For your site’s main menu, the location is typically called Primary, Header, Top, or Main Menu.
You may also see a [social links menu](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/social-links-menu/) which is another type of menu used for displaying links to your social media pages. Typically, you do not want to check this box for your main menu because it is a special menu used to [display social media icons](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/social-links-menu/#tab-customizer).
Name the menu and select the menu location
## Create multiple menus
You may want to create more than one menu for your site. For example, you may want a menu in your footer that shows different navigation links than the menu in your header. Or, you may want to create a sidebar with navigation to specific pages.
While [classic themes](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#classic-themes) have a predefined location for menus, you can create and add menus to any part of a [block theme](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#block-themes-recommended).
To create another menu in a [block theme](https://wordpress.com/support/theme-types/#block-themes-recommended), follow the steps below:
1. Visit your site’s dashboard.
2. Navigate to *Appearance →* *Editor.*
3. Click Templates or Patterns to select the [template](https://wordpress.com/support/templates/) or [template part](https://wordpress.com/support/template-part-block/) you want the navigation menu to appear.
4. Add the **Navigation block** where you want the new menu to appear. By default, it will load your existing menu. Or, if your template already has the menu you want to change, you can click the existing **Navigation block**.
5. In the sidebar settings on the right, where it says **Menu**, click on the three dots/ellipses icon.
If you do not have the sidebar on the right, click the Settings icon in the top-right corner to bring up the settings. This icon looks like a square with two uneven columns:
1. Click the “**Create new menu**” link in the drop-down menu. The existing menu will be removed from the page (but not completely deleted!), and a new, empty **Navigation block** will be added in its place.
2. Optional, but recommended: visit the **Settings** section of the Navigation block’s sidebar settings and click **Advanced** to give the menu a descriptive name.
3. Click the **+ Block Inserter** to begin [adding new menu items](https://wordpress.com/support/menus/add-links-to-a-menu/) to the **Navigation block**.
4. When you’ve finished adding the menu items, click the “**Save**” button in the top-right corner of the editor to update the page and save your new menu.
All of your existing menus are listed in the *Navigation* section of the Site Editor under *Appearance → Editor*.
## Create a menu with the AI assistant
> This section applies to sites created with the [AI Website Builder](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/) (all paid plans) or sites that have [enabled the AI assistant](https://wordpress.com/support/ai-website-builder/ai-tools/) (Business and Commerce plans). Open the AI assistant by clicking the AI icon (a four-pointed star) at the bottom of the screen.
The AI assistant can create new menus and add items to your site’s navigation directly. Here are some things you can ask it to do:
* “Create a new footer menu with links to Home, About, and Contact.”
* “Add a new menu item called Blog that links to /blog in the main navigation.”
* “Make the header and footer menus use the same set of links.”
* “Add a submenu under Schedule with items: Clinics, Camps, and 1‑on‑1 Sessions.”
