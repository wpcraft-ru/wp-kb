# Edit a site address
Last reviewed on March 17, 2026
Copy page
Your WordPress.com site comes with a free address like `yourgroovysite.wordpress.com`. You can change the first part (`yourgroovysite`) to make it more memorable and match your brand. In this guide, you will learn how to change your site address and understand your options.

## Understand your options
You have two main ways to customize your website’s address. Choose the option that best fits your needs:
**[Change your site address](https://wordpress.com/support/changing-site-address/#change-your-wordpress-com-site-address)** to update the first part of your free WordPress.com address. For example, change `yourgroovyaddress.wordpress.com` to `yournewgroovyaddress.wordpress.com`. This option is free and works best when you want a different name for your site and don’t mind the `wordpress.com` part of the site address.
⚠️
> Changing your site address will permanently break links to your old address. Your old address cannot be recovered or reused. Consider [adding a domain name instead](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/register-domain/) to keep your current address working.
**[Add a domain name](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/register-domain/)** for a completely custom web address. For example, change `yourgroovyaddress.wordpress.com` to `yourgroovydomain.com`. Domain names are included for free for the first year of any paid plan.
### Important things to know
Before changing your site address, understand these key points:
**You cannot reuse old addresses**
WordPress.com addresses that have been used before cannot be claimed again, even if the original site no longer exists. This protects content ownership and prevents impersonation.
**Plugin-enabled sites work differently**
If your site uses plugins, your address ends in `.wpcomstaging.com` instead of `.wordpress.com`. You cannot change the first part of a wpcomstaging address – you’ll need to [add a domain name instead](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/register-domain/).
## Change your WordPress.com site address
You can change the first part (e.g., `yourgroovysite.wordpress.com`) of your WordPress.com site address by following these steps:
1. Visit your [site’s dashboard](https://wordpress.com/home/).
2. On the left side, navigate to *Upgrades → Domains*.
3. Click the three dots to the far right of your WordPress.com site address, and select “**Change site address**“.
1. In the “**New site address**” field, type your desired address.
1. If the new address is available, click the **Next** button.
2. Review the new address and warning on the confirmation page.
1. Check the box next to **“I understand that I won’t be able to undo this change”** and click the **Confirm** button to complete the site address change.
Once you’ve completed the steps above, your site will immediately use the new WordPress.com address and stop using the old one.
## Add a domain name instead
Adding a domain name gives you a professional web address like `yourgroovysite.com` and keeps your current address working. This is often the better choice for established sites.
To add a domain name, follow these steps:
1. [Register a new domain name](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/register-domain/) or [connect a domain name you already own](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/connect-existing-domain/).
2. [Set your domain name as your primary address](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/set-a-primary-address/).
## Change a wpcomstaging.com address
If your site address ends in `.wpcomstaging.com`, you have a plugin-enabled site. A wpcomstaging address does not mean you have a staging site (you use a [staging site](https://wordpress.com/support/how-to-create-a-staging-site/) for testing). It is the temporary address for your plugin-enabled website until you add your own domain name.
**Example:** `yourgroovysite.wpcomstaging.com` ⁣becomes⁣ `yourgroovysite.com` when you add a domain name.
You cannot change the first part of a wpcomstaging address. Instead, you can [register](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/register-domain/), [connect](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/connect-existing-domain/), or [transfer](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/incoming-domain-transfer/) a domain name to use with your site.
After adding a domain name, [set it as your primary address](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/set-a-primary-address/) so visitors see your domain name instead of the wpcomstaging address.
**Note:** Anyone who previously used the old `.wordpress.com` version of your address can still reach your site — it automatically redirects to your current address.
Switching back to a `.wordpress.com` address removes all plugins, third-party themes, and related customizations from your site. [Contact our support team](https://wordpress.com/support/contact/) for help with this change.
## Frequently asked questions
### Can I use an address from my other WordPress.com site?
You can move a **domain name** between sites. Learn how to [move a domain name from one site to another](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/move-a-domain-to-another-wordpress-com-site/).
You cannot move a **site address** from one site to another, but you can move your content to the site that has the address you want.
If you want to move your content to the site that has the address you want to use, follow these steps:
1. [Export the content](https://wordpress.com/support/export/) from your current site.
2. [Import the content](https://wordpress.com/support/import/import-a-sites-content/) into the site with your preferred address.
3. Optionally, [redirect visitors](https://wordpress.com/support/site-redirect/) from your old site to the new one.
**Note:** This moves your content, not the address itself. The original address will remain with the site where it was created.
### Can I claim an address that someone else used?
No, WordPress.com addresses cannot be reused once they’ve been claimed, even if the original site is no longer active. This protects content ownership.
If someone else is using an address you want, you can ask them to [transfer the site to you](https://wordpress.com/support/transfer-a-site/). Contact them through a comment on their site or their contact form, if available. WordPress.com cannot contact site owners for you or provide their contact information.
Since there are unlimited available addresses, consider these alternatives:
* Try a variation of your desired name (add numbers, hyphens, or different words)
* [Register a custom domain name](https://wordpress.com/support/domains/register-domain/) like `yourgroovysite.com` instead
💡
> Domain names often look more professional than WordPress.com addresses and give you complete control over your web address.
